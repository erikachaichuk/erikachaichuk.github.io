Real estate market analysis

Aim

The real estate market is a complex and dynamic entity of a huge interest for investors and policymakers. Analyzing the real estate market is essential due to several reasons.


Firstly, such analysis provides with profitable investment opportunities and evaluates potential returns on properties for investors.


Secondly, analyzing how many properties are available versus sold on the market aids with identifying market imbalances.


The aim of this project is to conduct an analysis of the real estate market by leveraging data visualization techniques.


Specifically, this project focuses on:
1. Comparison of deal satisfaction by country on a scale from 0 to 5, with 0 strongly dissatisfied, while 5 – strongly satisfied.


2. Examination of age distribution within the market to understand the demographic composition of clients and its implications for the market targeting and segmentation.


3. Analysis of the geographical distribution of real estate clients in US, segmented by state, to identify state-level trends and opportunities.


4. Assessment of the total revenues of the real estate market from 2004 to 2010 inclusive to understand the overall market growth.


5. Track and visualization of the total number of sales per year by building type to reveal the demand patterns and preferences in property types.

Data and challanges

This project utilizes data from Statista. The corresponding csv files can be found on Github.


First, the properties data set was cleaned. The ID column was converted to string type to avoid unintentional numeric operations on the ID series. The column was also renamed to discard the irrelevant metadata.


Second, the customers data set was cleaned. There was an encoding issue with the ID column in the “properties”, and the first column in the “customers_raw_data” was also compromised. The column was thus renamed. Next, the entity column was turned from categoric to numeric by being renamed to ”individual” with values being mapped to 0 and 1. The “mortgage” variable was mapped to 0 and 1 as well.


Third, the two datasets were combined. That was possible through the “customer_ID” being the same feature in both tables. The initial merge resulted in missing some customer data because IDs in the “properties” table were flanked with empty spaces.


To overcome this problem, .strip() method was used, which removed the unnecessary spaces in both tables “customer_ID” series. The second attempt was successful, hence the joint table named “real estate data” was created. Before processing further, the data types of the combined table were verified.


The analysis phase began with calculating the age at purchase by taking it from the “birth_date” and “date_of_sale” fields.


Descriptive statistics and interval-based age groupings provided insights into the customers demographics. Property prices were similarly analyzed by grouping them into price intervals, getting the distribution of the sold and unsold properties.


To examine therelationship between age and property prices, covariance and correlation was utilized. The covariance revealed the extent of the relationship and whether the age impacts the price of properties. The correlation coefficient helped to explore the relationship further.


Geographical and categorical insights were explored through breakdowns and a scatterplot, looking further into California, since this state has the highest number of properties sold per year. For example, the properties were analyzed by building to see the trends in average price, area, and deal satisfaction. A state-level breakdown included frequency, relative frequency, and cumulative frequency calculations.

Limitations

Real estate market data is not always up-to-date, which caused the analyses to be based on outdated information. The data used also lacks niche markets and smaller geographic areas. It is thus not possible to predict the future, since the data relies on past performances and not the most accurate data.

Conclusions

Although not fully predictive and representative, this project has several insights.


Firstly, it has been seen that market trends are highly sensitive to economic cycles, with 2007 serving as a peak year before a sharp downturn due to external factors, specifically economic crisis in US.


It has also been seen that middle-aged buyers dominate the market, indicating a need to tailor services and strategies on the real estate market to this financially active group.


Moreover, markets with lower sales and satisfaction levels, such as Denmark or less populated US states, offer untapped potential for development and customer-focused improvements.


Lastly, as have been seen in California, which is the region with the highest number of apartments sold per year, the urban regions may remain the epicenter of activity in some cases, with prices increasing as one moves closer to coastal area.
